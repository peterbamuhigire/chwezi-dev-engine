# Access, localisation, and safety gates

For every critical gameplay action test discovery, activation, timing, precision, hold/repeat, remapping, one-handed/alternate input, feedback redundancy, interruption, and recovery. Record platform/device, input method, assist settings, observed barrier, severity, resolution, and tester basis.

Localisation gates include source freeze/version, context and screenshots, pseudo-localisation, missing/truncated/overflow text, glyph/font coverage, bidirectional layout when applicable, controller prompts, subtitle speaker/timing, voice fallback, culturally sensitive content, and save/network compatibility.

Safety gates cover default communication posture, consent, mute/block/report, rate limits, evidence minimisation, urgent-harm escalation, moderator tooling, consistency review, appeal, transparency, child/guardian controls where applicable, and failure when automated services are unavailable.

Game accessibility uses game-specific evidence alongside web/platform rules. Inspect HUD and diegetic information, menus/maps/inventory/dialogue/tutorials, failure/success and save/load states, couch distance, touch occlusion, focus order, remapping, time limits, difficulty assists, motion, photosensitivity, audio-only and colour-only cues, haptic fatigue and first-launch access to settings. Do not pass because a menu checklist or automated contrast scan passed.

For ads and purchases, verify clear identification, focus/input isolation, readable close/decline, no accidental-tap overlap, pause/resume and screen-reader behaviour, no critical information underneath, no interruption of play/learning/narrative/save/recovery/exit, age-appropriate creative and a tested no-fill/no-network/SDK-failure path. Child-directed or unknown-age delivery fails closed when age/privacy/creative controls are not proven.

Cultural review records claim or motif, provenance, fact/fiction treatment, reviewer/community relationship and scope, permission/rights decision, disagreement, required change and approval expiry. Research is not permission and one reviewer is not a universal community mandate.
