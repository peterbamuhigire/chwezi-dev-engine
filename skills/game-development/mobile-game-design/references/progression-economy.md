# Ethical progression and economy

Back to [Mobile Game Design](../SKILL.md).

Map sources, sinks, inventory caps, unlock prerequisites, loss, recovery, earn/spend cadence, non-paying path, paying path and regional/payment constraints. Version the economy and simulate representative player paths before release.

Progression should create new decisions through skill, capability, knowledge, relationships, expression or authored content. Numerical growth is acceptable only when it changes pacing or challenge meaningfully. Keep sacred, cultural and educational material out of disposable currency loops.

Reject pay-to-win, artificial frustration sold back as relief, misleading scarcity, undisclosed chance, child-directed pressure and retention based mainly on punishment. Verify current store, payment, tax and consumer rules.

## Advertising contract

Treat ads as an optional business-model hypothesis, not a default progression system. For every placement record: player-facing purpose, format, trigger, genuine break, explicit opt-in where used, decline/close path, reward and equivalence, cap, pace, age/territory treatment, creative controls, data/SDK purpose, offline/no-fill/error behaviour, remove-ads/no-ads posture, kill switch and evidence owner.

Block ads that disguise themselves as content, appear where a player expects a control, interrupt the requested action, punish declining, monetise a recent loss, block save/recovery/exit, create pay-to-win or grind pressure, or exploit a child or vulnerable player. A rewarded ad remains intrusive when the surrounding economy makes the choice coercive.

Pair ad impressions and revenue with interruption, decline, accidental-tap, complaint, refund, regret, session-exit, child-safety and trust signals. Do not set invented thresholds; pre-register product-specific stop rules and fail closed when required evidence or compliant delivery is unavailable.
